# Asking for Help

No matter how well you study, you will get stuck. Here is how to handle it gracefully.

## The Most Important Phrase

**Aš nesuprantu.** (I don't understand.)

- _Pronunciation:_ Ash ne-su-pran-**TU**.

Add a polite "Atsiprašau" (Excuse me/Sorry) before it:

- _Atsiprašau, aš nesuprantu._

## "Do You Speak English?"

Younger people in Vilnius/Kaunas almost always speak English. Older generations might speak Russian.

- **Ar kalbate angliškai?** (Do you speak English?)
- _Ar kalbate rusiškai?_ (Do you speak Russian?)

## Keywords for Confusion

| Lithuanian            | English              |
| :-------------------- | :------------------- |
| **Prašom pakartoti.** | Please repeat.       |
| **Lėčiau, prašom.**   | Slower, please.      |
| **Ką tai reiškia?**   | What does this mean? |

## Emergencies

If you are in real trouble, keeping it simple is fine.

- **Padėkite!** (Help me!)
- **Kvieskite policiją/greitąją.** (Call police/ambulance.)
- **Kur yra ligoninė?** (Where is the hospital?)

**Universal Emergency Number:** **112** (Works everywhere in EU).

## Quick Practice Scenarios

**Scenario 1: On the bus, someone speaks fast.**

- _You:_ "Atsiprašau, aš nesuprantu. Ar kalbate angliškai?"

**Scenario 2: You need to find the train station.**

- _You:_ "Atsiprašau, kur yra stotis?" (Excuse me, where is the station?)
